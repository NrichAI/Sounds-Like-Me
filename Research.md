## Sounds Like Me: Research Foundation

> Music preference is not random. It reflects deeper psychological, emotional, and social patterns that remain stable across time and culture. Decades of empirical research in music psychology have demonstrated consistent relationships between musical preference and personality traits.

## The Central Question

Can music preferences reveal personality structure?

Not merely correlate with it — but serve as a valid, non-invasive measurement instrument for the Big Five (OCEAN) personality dimensions?

The research literature demonstrates consistent and statistically significant correlations between music preferences and personality traits, particularly within the Big Five (OCEAN) framework.

## The Landmark Study

A major study conducted by Professor Adrian North at Heriot-Watt University surveyed **36,518 participants** worldwide, who rated their preferences across 104 musical styles and completed a Big Five personality inventory. At the time of publication — *Individual Differences in Musical Taste* — it was widely described as the largest study examining the relationship between musical taste and personality.

The study confirmed that personality traits correlate with musical preference in statistically meaningful ways. It also found that demographic variables including age, sex, and income sometimes showed relationships of comparable strength — an important nuance that the broader research literature acknowledges. Music preference is a meaningful psychological signal. It is one signal among several, and Sounds Like Me treats it as such.

## Why Music Works as a Psychological Signal

Most personality assessments rely on self-report — asking people to describe how they think, feel, and behave. The limitations are well-documented.

Musical response operates differently.

| Property | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **Implicit response** | Musical responses involve affective and neurological processing that often occurs before conscious reflection — they are not deliberately constructed answers |
| **Low self-report bias** | People are significantly less likely to manipulate musical taste than questionnaire responses |
| **Identity relevance** | North established that music functions as a *badge of identity* — preferences reflect self-concept rather than performance of it |
| **Temporal stability** | Musical preferences show substantial stability across time, comparable to other lifestyle indicators linked to personality |
| **Cross-cultural replication** | Core psychological relationships between music and personality hold across populations, even where genre meanings vary |

> Preferences often emerge automatically rather than through deliberate self-description. Personality traits influence what individuals tend to find resonant or engaging in music — making musical response a more naturalistic signal than direct self-report.

## Music-Personality Correlations: The Core Findings

The following correlations are drawn from North's published research and replicated across the broader music psychology literature. These are statistical relationships — probabilistic, not deterministic.

### Openness

High Openness correlates with preference for **complex, unconventional music** — jazz, classical, world music, avant-garde, and experimental genres.

Low Openness correlates with preference for conventional, mainstream genres.

*Mechanism:* Openness predicts tolerance for ambiguity and appreciation of structural complexity. Classical and jazz listeners in North's research scored consistently high on self-esteem and creativity — a finding that challenged assumptions about genre and social identity.

### Extraversion

High Extraversion correlates with **upbeat, energetic, socially oriented music** — pop, dance, hip-hop.

Low Extraversion correlates with introspective, quieter genres.

*Mechanism:* Extraverts seek external stimulation to reach optimal arousal. Introverts use calmer music to maintain focus without overstimulation. North's arousal regulation research established this as a functional rather than purely aesthetic preference.

### Conscientiousness

High Conscientiousness correlates with **structured, traditional genres** — country, religious music, conventional pop.

Low Conscientiousness correlates with rebellious, unconventional genres.

*Mechanism:* Conscientious individuals prefer order and respect established conventions — a tendency that extends to musical structure and genre associations.

### Agreeableness

High Agreeableness correlates with **emotionally warm, gentle music** — folk, singer-songwriter, acoustic genres.

Low Agreeableness correlates with aggressive or confrontational music.

*Mechanism:* Agreeable individuals seek harmony and interpersonal warmth — preferences that extend to musical texture and emotional tone.

### Neuroticism

High Neuroticism correlates with **emotionally intense, cathartic music** — emo, heavy metal, melancholic ballads.

Low Neuroticism correlates with upbeat, emotionally stable genres.

*Mechanism:* Individuals high in Neuroticism use music as an emotional regulation tool — seeking catharsis or resonance rather than mood elevation.

### A Notable Finding: Heavy Metal and Classical

North's research produced one of its most counterintuitive findings in the comparison between heavy metal and classical listeners. Despite the stark contrast in sonic character, both groups scored similarly on creativity, openness, and emotional depth. Genre surface does not reliably predict the psychological profile of the listener — the structural and emotional properties of the music are the meaningful signal.

## The Assessment Methodology

### Why 45 Tracks

Statistical power. Each track rating adds signal across the personality dimensions. Individual noise — tracks rated unusually due to mood, familiarity, or context — cancels out across the full set. 45 tracks balances measurement reliability against completion time.

### Why 30-Second Clips

Genre recognition and preference formation occur within seconds. Research shows that listeners form stable responses to musical stimuli within 15–30 seconds of exposure. Full tracks add time without adding meaningful signal.

### What Is Being Measured

Sounds Like Me does not analyse listening history. It does not track what you have heard before or how often you listen to a given artist.

It presents controlled audio stimuli — tracks selected to span genre, complexity, energy, and emotional register — and measures how you respond. You are not required to recognise the tracks. Prior familiarity is not a factor in the assessment design.

Each response is mapped against psychometric correlations derived from North's validated research. Across 45 responses, personality patterns emerge.

---

## Cultural Specificity

### The Problem With Global Averages

Genre meanings are culturally situated. Country music carries different connotations in Nashville than in London. Classical music holds different cultural weight across populations. Applying a single global playlist and a single set of norms produces a flattened, culturally invalid signal.

### The Solution: Culture-Specific Research Infrastructure

Sounds Like Me uses separate playlists, separate genre-personality coefficients, and separate normative data for different cultural contexts.

| Component | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Culture-specific playlists** | Track sets calibrated to local genre distributions and cultural meaning |
| **Culture-specific coefficients** | Personality correlations derived from local research samples, not global averages |
| **Culture-specific percentile norms** | Your scores compare you to people in your cultural context |

> A 70th percentile Openness score means you score higher than 70% of people in your country — a culturally grounded comparison, not a statistical abstraction.

This approach directly applies North's research principle that musical meaning must be interpreted within its cultural context.

## What This Is Not

Sounds Like Me does not use machine learning algorithms to predict personality from listening behaviour.

The assessment is grounded in validated psychological instruments and real population samples derived from North's research program. Results represent where your preference patterns sit relative to established psychometric norms — not a proprietary algorithmic output applied to behavioural data.

Personality is complex. OCEAN dimensions are continuous, not categorical. Sounds Like Me presents results as a lens for self-reflection and as a foundation for adaptive AI interaction — not as a definitive psychological classification.

## Psychometric Mapping

Music preference ratings are mapped to personality dimensions using weighted correlations derived from published research in music psychology. Each track contributes probabilistic signal across multiple personality dimensions simultaneously.

The general form of the mapping is:

```
O_score = Σ(track_rating × openness_weight)
C_score = Σ(track_rating × conscientiousness_weight)
E_score = Σ(track_rating × extraversion_weight)
A_score = Σ(track_rating × agreeableness_weight)
N_score = Σ(track_rating × neuroticism_weight)
```

Where weights are derived from validated genre-personality correlations calibrated to the user's cultural norm group.

Across the full 45-track assessment, individual responses aggregate into a stable personality vector representing the user's relative position across all five OCEAN dimensions. Scores are expressed as percentiles against the relevant cultural norm group — not as raw totals or categorical labels.

No single track is deterministic. The signal emerges from the pattern across the full assessment.

## Report Generation

Personality reports are produced by **SLM-3** — a proprietary model developed and fine-tuned by NRich specifically for OCEAN-based personality analysis. SLM-3 operates exclusively on the structured OCEAN vector output from the assessment. It does not process personal data or conversation content.

General-purpose language models are not optimised for psychometric interpretation. SLM-3 is trained on the specific task of translating OCEAN profiles into structured, meaningful personality reports — producing analysis with the precision the methodology requires.
